# Finance Models: Case Study

This repository contains **reusable financial models** built for **40+ investment projects** across SMEs, startups, and corporate ventures in real estate, infrastructure, retail, manufacturing, and SaaS.

## Business Context

Many small and medium businesses in Russia lacked formal financial discipline:

- No standardized templates for **DCF**, **LBO**, or **scenario‑based budgeting**.
- Ad‑hoc Excel spreadsheets with inconsistent structures and unclear assumptions.
- Difficulty to **defend** projects in front of investors, banks, or partners.

As a **financial analyst and CFO‑level professional**, I created a set of **standardized, flexible financial models** to:

- Accelerate project analysis,
- Reduce errors,
- Communicate assumptions and risks transparently.

## Solution Overview

The `Finance Models` repository provides templates for:

- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** – valuation of investments.
- **Leveraged Buyout (LBO)** – debt‑financed acquisitions.
- **Scenario‑based budgeting** – optimistic, base, and pessimistic cases.
- **KPI dashboards** – key metrics (NPV, IRR, equity multiple, payback, etc.).

These templates are:

- **Reusable** across industries,
- **Customizable** for specific projects,
- **Clear on assumptions** and risks.

## Typical Use Cases

### 1. Real Estate & Infrastructure Projects

- Developers and construction companies use **DCF models** to:
  - Estimate **required investments**,
  - Project **cashflows** over 5–10 years,
  - Calculate **NPV, IRR, payback period**,
  - Test **sensitivity to rent, occupancy, and construction delays**.

### 2. Startups and SaaS

- Founders use **scenario‑based budgets** to:
  - Model **revenue growth**, **burn rate**, and **cash runway**,
  - Design **path to profitability**,
  - Prepare for **seed and Series A rounds**.

### 3. Manufacturing & Retail

- Owners and managers use **operational budgets** and **KPI dashboards** to:
  - Track **gross margin, EBITDA, and net profit**,
  - Monitor **inventory, AR, and AP**,
  - Plan **working capital** needs.

## Technical Implementation

The models are built in:

- **Excel** (for corporate users),
- **Google Sheets** (for collaboration and remote teams).

Each model follows a strict structure:

- **Inputs worksheet** – explicit assumptions,
- **Calculations** – clear formulas, no hidden macros,
- **Outputs** – summary tables and charts.

This ensures:

- **Transparency**,
- **Ease of audit**,
- **Fast adaptation** to new projects.
